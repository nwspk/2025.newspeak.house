---
title: Nick Botti
---

Nicholas is the former head of AI tech and innovation at the US Federal Reserve. In that role he published research evaluating the effectiveness and failure modes of AI for financial stability analyses. He also developed economic simulation projects, advised policymakers on the economic implications of artificial intelligence, and led culture change around technology delivery and innovation in government.

His research interests include: how societies and institutions respond and adapt to technological change, the design and implementation of markets and incentive structures, the systemic factors that influence cooperation vs conflict, non-linear dynamical systems modeling, the influence of technology on social and psychological dynamics, and the intersection of attention, autonomy, and community in the modern world.

He’s particularly interested in exploring the “blind spots” of data-driven decision making - how they fail to capture the intangible, the immeasurable, and the black swan events inherent in a non-linear world, and is focused on applications of these areas to community and institutional design, climate change, and AI safety/alignment.

Nicholas holds degrees in mathematics and economics from Dickinson College.
